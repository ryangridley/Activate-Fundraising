02 How It Works

The donation flow runs in two stages.

Attribution is captured BEFORE the donor reaches Stripe's checkout. After payment, a webhook reconciles the order back to the staged record — writing the donation, attributions, and updating cached totals in one transactional sequence.

Stage 1

Before Checkout

Platform-controlled — full attribution captured

  • 01Donor opens school page (or arrives via share link)
  • 02Selects teacher → student → adds students
  • 03Enters amount; optional 3% + $0.30 fee coverage
  • 04CAPTCHA clears; frontend captures session token
  • 05Backend creates pending_donation with allocations JSON
  • 06Backend creates Stripe checkout session
Stage 2

After Payment

Webhook-driven — match pending → write donation + attributions

  • 01Donor pays at Stripe (hosted checkout)
  • 02Stripe webhook fires → Express.js endpoint
  • 03Match by sessionToken (primary) or email + amount (fallback)
  • 04Insert donation record (with breakdownText, fees)
  • 05Loop allocations → insert attributions, update raised totals
  • 06Update school total + share link → mark COMPLETED
Why sessionToken is primary: donors routinely change emails or add tips at Stripe checkout. The sessionToken is captured at staging and persists through the order, making it a stable matching key.
Architecture in layers

Stripe is the payment system of record. PostgreSQL is the attribution and reporting system of record. The two are deliberately kept separate.

Frontend
React / Next.js on DigitalOcean App Platform
Pages, components, donor flow, dashboards
Payments
Stripe Donations + Stripe Payments
Nonprofit-compliant checkout — handles cards & methods
Backend Logic
Node.js (12 modules) on DigitalOcean
Validation, attribution, reconciliation, permissions
Data
PostgreSQL (15 tables) on DigitalOcean
Attribution, ledger, directory, prizes, shares, audit
Identity
Custom Auth + Households
Email-normalized donor identity & transactional email routing
Webhook + Email
Express.js Webhooks + SendGrid
Processes completed orders; database-triggered donor email
Five principles that drive every architectural decision.
P1

Stripe = payments of record

Custom database is the attribution and reporting source of truth — never commingled with the payment ledger.

P2

Database is authoritative

Frontend logic is never trusted. All writes happen in Node.js backend modules with server-side validation.

P3

Attribution is never optional

Every donation is tied to a school, teacher, and one or more students before checkout proceeds.

P4

Totals are real-time computed

Stored "raised" fields are convenience caches. They can drift, and reconciliation is a supported activity.

P5

Infrastructure you own & control

DigitalOcean gives full access to logs, scaling controls, and deployment pipelines — no platform lock-in.

03 Phase 2

A parent-centric expansion. Additive only.

Phase 2 brings parents into their own portal, gives them control over share contacts and outreach, and adds reminder automations for opted-in households. No Phase 2 feature modifies the donation flow, attribution rules, or financial ledger.

👤

Parent Member Portal

Authenticated household dashboard with linked students, household totals, active share links, recent donation visibility, and clear next-step prompts. Built on DigitalOcean with JWT auth — unauthenticated users are redirected to login.

⏱

Household Settings

Individual vs Equal-Split fundraising mode without altering historical donations.

📈

Outreach Event Tracking

Send, click, donate, and engagement states tracked via additive database collections and reporting-safe event logs.

✉️

Automations & Reminders

Parent reminders, dashboard nudges, and follow-up messaging via SendGrid Triggered Emails.

💬

Share Contacts

Add, edit, archive, and track outreach contacts by household.

🔧

Admin Support Visibility

Deeper visibility into the customer journey — household-level lookup, a complete outreach timeline per parent, and the ability to act on edge cases without going to engineering.
